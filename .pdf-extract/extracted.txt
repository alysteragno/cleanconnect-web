ABSTRACT 
This study presents the design and development of CleanConnect, an AI-enhanced web 
and mobile application developed to support the operational management of Cleaning 
Lady PH, a multi-branch cleaning service provider in the Philippines. The system 
integrates essential business functions such as service booking, cleaner scheduling, 
manpower estimation, payment tracking, and customer feedback into a unified platform. 
Customers and cleaners primarily interact with the system through the mobile 
application, while the web application is used for company information, service details, 
announcements, and administrative content management. 
The system was developed to address operational challenges caused by fragmented tools 
and manual processes, including delayed booking responses, inefficient cleaner 
deployment, and difficulty in managing records across multiple branches. CleanConnect 
applies artificial intelligence to assist in manpower estimation and scheduling by 
considering cleaner availability, service requirements, and assigned day-offs. This 
approach helps reduce manual errors, improve coordination, and support timely service 
delivery. 
CleanConnect benefits customers by providing a convenient and organized method for 
requesting cleaning services, monitoring booking status, and submitting feedback. 
Cleaners benefit from structured job assignments and clear work schedules, while 
administrators gain access to centralized records and decision support for managing 
operations. The system supports more efficient coordination across branches and 
contributes to improved service reliability. 
The developmental research methodology was employed to guide the analysis, design, 
development, and testing of the system. Through this study, the researchers enhanced 
their skills in system development and gained practical experience in building a 
user-centered information system. The study demonstrates the value of intelligent 
systems in improving operational efficiency within service-oriented organizations.

INTRODUCTION 
The rapid growth of digital and on-demand service platforms has changed how 
service-based businesses manage their operations and interact with customers. Cleaning 
service companies are increasingly expected to provide fast booking processes, accurate 
scheduling, and reliable service delivery through online systems. According to Laudon 
and Laudon (2020), modern information systems help organizations integrate business 
processes, reduce manual work, and improve operational efficiency. These systems are 
especially important in labor-intensive service industries, where workforce coordination 
and time management directly affect service quality. 
The AI-Enhanced Cleaning Service Operations System is designed to address the 
operational needs of cleaning service providers by offering a centralized web and mobile 
platform for managing bookings, scheduling, payments, and cleaner deployment. Digital 
reservation systems allow customers to conveniently request services and receive timely 
confirmations, which improves overall customer experience. Studies on service 
automation show that technology-enabled self-service platforms enhance customer 
satisfaction by improving speed, accessibility, and accuracy of service delivery (Huang & 
Rust, 2018). 
From an operational perspective, the proposed system integrates all service data 
into a centralized database that supports real-time monitoring and coordination. 
Centralized service management systems have been shown to improve visibility and 
2 
 

 
control in multi-branch organizations, particularly those managing mobile workforces 
(Turban, Pollard, & Wood, 2018). By consolidating booking records, cleaner schedules, 
customer data, and payment history, the system helps reduce errors caused by fragmented 
tools and manual processes. 
A key feature of the proposed system is the use of artificial intelligence to support 
manpower estimation, cleaner availability checking, and scheduling optimization. 
Research indicates that AI-based decision support systems improve workforce allocation 
by analyzing service demand, employee availability, and operational constraints (Wamba 
et al., 2021). In service operations, intelligent scheduling systems help reduce double 
bookings, minimize idle time, and improve overall productivity, making them particularly 
suitable for cleaning service companies with multiple service locations. 
The system is developed specifically for Cleaning Lady PH, a multi-branch 
cleaning service company operating in Manila, Cebu, Baguio, and through franchise 
partners. The company currently relies on disconnected platforms such as social media 
messaging, spreadsheets, and separate payment systems, which leads to delayed 
responses, scheduling conflicts, and inefficient cleaner deployment. Studies on digital 
transformation emphasize that fragmented systems limit scalability and reduce an 
organization’s ability to respond effectively to customer demand (Laudon & Laudon, 
2020). 
The rationale for implementing the AI-Enhanced Cleaning Service Operations 
System lies in its relevance to the growing demands of cleaning service companies, its 
3 
 

 
practical application to real-world operational challenges, and its potential impact on both 
business performance and customer satisfaction. As Cleaning Lady PH continues to 
expand its operations, the need for an integrated and intelligent system becomes essential 
to improve operational efficiency, ensure fair and accurate cleaner deployment, and 
enhance service reliability through automated scheduling, centralized records, and 
AI-supported decision-making. 
1.1.   Purpose and Description 
CleanConnect addresses these issues by integrating essential operational 
components into a unified platform. Administrators can manage bookings, track cleaner 
availability, oversee deployment planning, and monitor payments within a single 
interface. The system also supports structured communication channels for inquiries and 
feedback, enabling more organized interactions among customers, cleaners, and 
administrators. Additional features such as a built‑in chatbox, complaint reporting, 
automated notifications, and geo-tagging further enhance communication, transparency, 
and tracking throughout the service process. 
A key feature of the proposed system is its incorporation of a rule‑based artificial 
intelligence mechanism designed to support manpower planning and scheduling. The AI 
evaluates operational factors such as cleaner availability, approved rest days, service 
requirements, workload distribution, branch assignment, and estimated travel time to 
assist in determining appropriate cleaner deployment. Through this structured 
4 
 

 
decision-support logic, the system aims to improve scheduling accuracy, prevent double 
bookings, and maintain consistent service delivery across multiple branches. 
Overall, CleanConnect is designed to enhance the operational coordination of 
Cleaning Lady PH by providing a centralized, technology-supported platform that 
promotes efficient service management, accurate scheduling, and reliable payment 
tracking. By improving the organization of operational data and establishing more 
structured workflows, the system is expected to contribute to higher service reliability 
and a more seamless experience for both administrators and customers. 
 
1.2. Project Context 
Cleaning Lady PH began its operations in Metro Manila in 2016 and has since 
expanded to multiple regions across the Philippines. The company established its Cebu 
branch in 2021 and later expanded to Baguio in 2023, while also operating through 
franchise partners in Quezon City and Muntinlupa. As a professional home-cleaning 
service provider, Cleaning Lady PH offers a range of services including general home 
cleaning, premium mattress and upholstery cleaning, complete home cleaning packages 
with steam sterilization, disinfection services, and post‑construction cleaning. On 
average, more than twenty cleaners are deployed daily to serve residential and 
commercial clients. Over nearly a decade of operations, the company has maintained a 
steady demand for its services while providing meaningful livelihood opportunities to 
5 
 

 
many workers, particularly mothers and older women who rely on the company as a 
primary source of income. 
Despite its growth, Cleaning Lady PH continues to rely on several independent 
and unintegrated tools to manage its operations. Customer inquiries and bookings are 
primarily received through social media channels such as Facebook, Instagram, Viber, 
and WhatsApp, while the company website is used occasionally but often experiences 
delays. Payment processing is handled through PayMongo, accounting records are 
managed using Xero, and booking details, schedules, and deployment notes are 
maintained manually through Google Sheets and shared calendars. Because these tools 
operate separately, staff members must repeatedly transfer information between 
platforms, cross-check schedules, and manually consolidate operational records. These 
fragmented processes increase administrative workload, introduce potential errors in 
booking and deployment coordination, and make it difficult to track customer feedback 
and monitor service performance consistently. 
To systematically assess these operational challenges, this study applies an 
Ishikawa or cause‑and‑effect framework using the 6M model—Manpower, Methods, 
Machines, Materials (Data), Measurement, and Environment. From a manpower 
perspective, cleaner scheduling and deployment depend heavily on manual coordination, 
increasing the likelihood of human error, missed inquiries, and double bookings. In terms 
of methods, the absence of standard booking workflows and centralized documentation 
slows down decision-making and weakens coordination across branches and franchise 
partners. From a technological standpoint, the lack of a unified platform results in 
6 
 

 
fragmented communication and minimal real-time visibility across operations. Data 
management is also affected, as customer profiles, service histories, payment logs, and 
feedback records are scattered across separate tools. Operational metrics such as cleaner 
availability, service durations, and workload distribution are tracked manually, limiting 
the organization’s ability to use data for accurate analysis and planning. These challenges 
are further intensified by multi-city operations, franchise expansions, and growing 
customer expectations in a competitive digital service market. 
In response to these operational limitations, this study introduces CleanConnect, 
an AI-enhanced unified cleaning service operations system designed to streamline and 
support the core processes of Cleaning Lady PH. The proposed platform integrates 
booking management, manpower estimation, cleaner scheduling, deployment 
coordination, payment logging, real-time messaging, complaints management, and 
centralized record storage into a single web- and mobile-based system. Its rule-based 
artificial intelligence module assists in manpower planning and schedule generation by 
analyzing cleaner availability, service requirements, branch assignments, cleaner rest 
days, and estimated travel times using geo‑tagging. Through centralized data storage, 
real-time monitoring, structured communication tools, and automated notifications, the 
system enables administrators to manage operations more efficiently while improving 
accuracy, transparency, and coordination across branches. 
The significance of this project lies in its direct response to the critical operational 
challenges faced by Cleaning Lady PH. By consolidating previously fragmented 
workflows into one coherent digital platform, CleanConnect aims to enhance scheduling 
7 
 

 
accuracy, improve cleaner deployment efficiency, and strengthen operational 
coordination across multiple locations. Ultimately, the system supports the organization’s 
long-term scalability and reinforces its mission of empowering women and older workers 
through stable, technology-supported employment and improved service reliability.

mechanisms—such as availability checking, workload balancing, proximity matching, 
and buffer-time computation—the proposed system aims to enhance scheduling accuracy, 
improve operational coordination, and strengthen service reliability. Ultimately, the 
system supports organizational scalability, improves customer experience, and reinforces 
Cleaning Lady PH’s mission of delivering efficient, transparent, and technology-enabled 
cleaning services.

1.4. Scope and Delimitations 
This study focuses on the design and development of CleanConnect, a fully 
functional AI‑enhanced cleaning service operations system created for Cleaning Lady 
PH, a multi‑branch cleaning service provider operating in Manila, Cebu, Baguio, and 
selected franchise locations in the Philippines. The system is developed as a 
production-ready web and mobile application that centralizes core operational processes 
such as customer registration, service booking, manpower estimation, cleaner scheduling, 
deployment management, payment logging, real‑time messaging, feedback monitoring, 
and complaint reporting within a unified cloud-based environment. 
12 
 

 
The scope of the system includes the implementation of a rule-based artificial 
intelligence decision-support mechanism that evaluates service requirements, property 
characteristics, cleaner availability, branch assignments, approved rest days, existing 
schedules, and workload distribution prior to booking confirmation. The AI computes 
estimated manpower, validates available slots, calculates buffer times, and filters eligible 
cleaners based on operational constraints. Scheduling is enhanced through distance-based 
ETA computation using geo-tagged cleaner and client locations. Deployment follows an 
acceptance-based model, where eligible cleaners receive ranked booking offers. If no 
cleaner accepts the job, administrators may manually override assignments to ensure 
uninterrupted service operations. 
CleanConnect provides comprehensive operational tools through its integrated 
modules, including cleaner profile management, job history tracking, customer account 
management, service logs, digitized payment records, geo-tagging, in-app messaging, 
automated notifications, and complaints management. Multi-branch operational access is 
supported through a centralized cloud database that provides administrators with 
synchronized booking records, deployment updates, and branch-level reporting. 
This study includes the complete phases of requirements analysis, system design, 
development, testing, and deployment of a fully working web and mobile application 
intended for actual use by Cleaning Lady PH. The system will be turned over at the end 
of Capstone 2 with full operational features as specified in the project requirements. 
13 
 

 
However, the study maintains certain boundaries. It does not include advanced 
machine learning models, predictive analytics, dynamic route optimization, real-time 
GPS movement tracking, automated payroll computation, inventory management, offline 
functionality, or full third‑party accounting integration beyond payment logging. The 
artificial intelligence component is limited to structured rule‑based logic and does not 
employ data‑trained models. System performance depends on accurate user-provided data 
and is subject to external conditions such as internet connectivity, device compatibility, 
GPS signal quality, and payment gateway availability. While the system is designed 
specifically for Cleaning Lady PH, further customization may be required for adoption by 
other organizations.

The development of CleanConnect, an AI‑Enhanced Cleaning Service Operations 
System for Cleaning Lady PH, is significant as it directly addresses the organization’s

centralized record management to improve operational efficiency and service accuracy.

Cleaning Lady PH benefits from the system through automated manpower

messaging, structured complaint reporting, and consistent status updates contribute to

Cleaners also gain access to organized records of their job history, performance ratings, 
and schedule, enhancing accountability and workplace transparency. 
Future researchers and developers benefit from this study as it provides a 
reference model for designing AI‑enhanced service management systems, particularly 
those focused on multi‑branch operations, real-time coordination, and rule‑based 
automation. The study contributes to academic discussions on AI‑driven workforce 
15 
 

 
management, digital transformation in service industries, and the integration of 
operational decision-support mechanisms in small to medium enterprises. 
Overall, this study is significant because it demonstrates how an integrated, 
AI‑supported system can transform scattered manual workflows into a streamlined, 
reliable, and scalable operational model. CleanConnect not only improves efficiency and 
decision-making for Cleaning Lady PH but also reinforces the organization’s mission of 
empowering women and older workers through stable and technology-supported 
employment. 
 
1.6. Conceptual Framework 
This study’s conceptual framework outlines the structure used in developing the 
CleanConnect AI-Enhanced Cleaning Service Operations System for Cleaning Lady PH. 
It describes how the different components of the study are systematically arranged and 
linked to produce the intended system output. The framework adopts the 
Input–Process–Output (IPO) model to illustrate how foundational resources are 
transformed through structured development activities into a functional web and mobile 
platform for managing cleaning service operations. Through this model, the progression 
of the study from system preparation to implementation is clearly presented. 
16 
 

 
 
Figure 2 Conceptual Framework 
The Input component represents the resources necessary for system development 
and is classified into knowledge, hardware, and software requirements. These inputs 
provide the technical and conceptual groundwork for the creation of the CleanConnect 
system. The knowledge requirements include competencies in system analysis and 
design, web and mobile application development, database management, and UI/UX 
17 
 

 
design, as well as familiarity with cleaning service workflows. Hardware requirements 
consist of development computers, mobile devices, and reliable internet connectivity. 
Software requirements include appropriate programming languages and frameworks, 
database management systems, and development tools used throughout the system 
creation and testing phases. 
The Process component covers the activities that convert the identified inputs into 
a working system. It begins with system analysis and design, where requirements are 
gathered, workflows are examined, and system architecture and interfaces are planned. 
This stage is followed by system development, which involves building the web and 
mobile application modules and integrating the necessary database functionalities. After 
development, system testing is conducted to evaluate functionality and usability, along 
with identifying and correcting system errors. The final step in the process is system 
evaluation, which assesses the developed system based on selected software quality 
standards. 
The Output component of the IPO framework is CleanConnect, a web and 
mobile-based AI-enhanced cleaning service operations system. The system offers 
centralized management of bookings, scheduling, cleaner deployment, and digital 
records, while supporting multi-branch operations. As an academic prototype, 
CleanConnect demonstrates the practical application of integrated information systems in 
improving operational efficiency and service delivery within cleaning service 
organizations.

1.7. Definition of Terms 
A. Technical Terms: 
Web and Mobile Platform – A software system accessible through both web browsers 
and mobile devices that allows data processing and user interaction. 
Artificial Intelligence (AI) – A computational technology that enables systems to 
analyze data, recognize patterns, and support automated decision-making. 
Centralized Database – A single structured data storage system where all operational 
records are stored and managed in one location. 
Automation – The use of software to perform tasks without manual human intervention. 
Decision Support System (DSS) – A computer-based system that analyzes data to assist 
management in making informed decisions. 
System Integration – The process of connecting multiple functions (booking, payment, 
scheduling, records) into one unified platform. 
Real-Time Monitoring – The ability to view and track system activities and service 
status as they happen. 
Algorithmic Scheduling – A rule-based or AI-driven method for assigning tasks to 
workers based on predefined conditions. 
Data-Driven Decision Making – Using system-generated data and analytics to guide 
operational decisions. 
19 
 

 
User Interface (UI) – The visual design and interactive components through which users 
interact with the system. 
Prototype System – A preliminary version of the system built for testing and evaluation 
rather than full commercial use. 
Cloud-Based System (implied in your study) – A system that stores and processes data 
over the internet rather than on local computers. 
B. Operational Terms :  
CleanConnect – The web and mobile platform developed in this study to manage 
bookings, scheduling, payments, cleaner deployment, and records for Cleaning Lady PH. 
AI-Enhanced Cleaning Service Operations System – The integrated system that uses 
artificial intelligence to assist in manpower estimation, scheduling, and cleaner 
deployment for Cleaning Lady PH. 
Booking Management – The process of receiving, processing, and confirming customer 
cleaning service requests through the system. 
Cleaner Deployment – The assignment and dispatch of cleaners to specific jobs based

3.2.5 System Architecture 
 
Figure 20. System Architecture of the System 
66 
 

 
Figure 20 illustrates the three-tier system architecture of CleanConnect, consisting 
of the Presentation Layer, Application Layer, and Data Layer. The backend hosts core 
modules for booking, scheduling, deployment, and reporting, including a rule-based AI 
Decision Module that performs manpower estimation, availability filtering, conflict 
detection with buffer-time validation, and ranked cleaner assignment. The centralized 
cloud database stores all operational records, while notifications provide booking status 
updates and communicate a structured two-hour arrival window upon confirmation. 
CleanConnect follows a three-tier architecture composed of the Presentation 
Layer, Application Layer, and Data Layer. The presentation layer consists of the 
Customer Mobile App, Cleaner Mobile App, and the Admin Web Portal. All client 
interfaces communicate with the application layer through secured API requests, ensuring 
consistent and synchronized booking, scheduling, and deployment operations across all 
branches. 
The application layer hosts the system’s core backend services responsible for 
booking management, user and cleaner profile management, payment logging, feedback 
and complaints handling, reporting, and multi-branch access control. Within this layer is 
the AI Decision Module (Rule-Based) that executes key intelligence functions such as 
manpower estimation, availability filtering, workload balancing, schedule conflict 
detection, and buffer-time validation. When a customer submits booking details (service 
type, space type, and total sqm), the AI Decision Module validates feasibility and 
generates eligible time slots and cleaner recommendations before the booking is 
67 
 

 
finalized. After booking confirmation, the system broadcasts booking offers to ranked 
eligible cleaners and proceeds with acceptance-based deployment. 
The data layer contains a centralized cloud database that stores all operational 
records including accounts, bookings, schedules, deployments, payments, feedback, 
complaints, and branch data. The backend performs create-read-update-delete operations 
through transactional processing to ensure data integrity, prevent duplicate scheduling, 
and support traceable reporting. A notification service in the application layer delivers 
status updates to users (e.g., booking submitted, booking accepted, schedule confirmed, 
job in progress, job completed). Upon booking confirmation, the system assigns a 
structured two-hour arrival window to the assigned cleaner to accommodate preparation 
and travel, and the estimated arrival timeframe is communicated to the customer through 
booking status notifications. 
The CleanConnect system integrates several application programming interfaces 
(APIs) to support its core functionalities. The Google Maps API is utilized for 
geo-location services, enabling the system to capture user coordinates, perform distance 
calculations, display maps, and support proximity-based cleaner assignment. This 
geo-location feature is combined with the system’s logic to ensure that the nearest 
qualified cleaner—based on specialization, availability, and proximity—is selected for 
each booking. For secure and cashless transactions, the system uses the PayMongo API, 
which facilitates in-app payments through GCash and card options using a payment intent 
approach. To handle backend operations, real-time data synchronization, and messaging, 
the system incorporates Firebase services, including Firebase Firestore or Realtime 
68 
 

 
Database for storing booking details, service types, area size, and customer notes, 
Firebase Authentication for secure user login and role management, and Firebase Cloud 
Messaging (FCM) for sending push notifications such as booking updates and reminders. 
In addition to these APIs, the system employs a custom rule-based AI mechanism 
to automate cleaner assignment, manpower estimation, and service scheduling. This 
mechanism utilizes predefined conditions including service type, geo-tagged location, 
area size (in square meters), type of space, and special customer requests, combined with 
cleaner-specific attributes such as specialization, availability, and distance from the 
service location. Through this approach, the system filters cleaners based on 
qualification, then selects the nearest available candidate, ensuring efficient, accurate, and 
consistent decision-making without manual intervention.

 3.3.1 Scrum Methodology 
 
Figure 39. Scrum Methodology Diagram  
 The development of CleanConnect: An AI-Enhanced Cleaning Service 
Operations System for Cleaning Lady PH followed the Agile Software Development Life 
Cycle (SDLC) implemented using the Scrum framework. Scrum was selected due to its 
structured yet flexible approach to iterative development, which is highly suitable for 
systems that require continuous refinement, particularly those integrating AI-supported 
scheduling and manpower estimation components. The framework allowed incremental 
system delivery while incorporating stakeholder feedback throughout the development 
process. 
Scrum organizes development into time-boxed iterations known as sprints. Each 
sprint produced a working system increment that progressively enhanced CleanConnect’s 
functionality. The Scrum framework defined specific roles to ensure organized workflow 
89 
 

 
and accountability. The Project Manager functioned as the Product Owner, responsible 
for defining and prioritizing system requirements in the product backlog. The 
development team, composed of system analysts, developers, and database 
administrators, was responsible for implementing system features. A designated Scrum 
facilitator ensured that sprint activities followed structured timelines and objectives. 
The development process began with the creation of a Product Backlog, which 
contained all identified system requirements including booking management, AI-based 
manpower estimation, scheduling automation, cleaner deployment, buffer-time 
computation, payment tracking, complaints management, and multi-branch support. Each 
requirement was prioritized based on operational importance and system dependencies. 
At the start of every sprint, Sprint Planning was conducted to select specific 
backlog items for implementation. These selected tasks formed the Sprint Backlog. The 
development team then proceeded with system design refinement, coding, database 
configuration, and integration within the sprint duration. 
Daily Scrum meetings were conducted to monitor progress, identify technical 
challenges, and ensure alignment with sprint goals. This practice enhanced coordination 
among developers and minimized development bottlenecks. 
At the end of each sprint, a Sprint Review was conducted to present the working 
system increment. Functional components such as booking workflows, cleaner 
assignment logic, AI scheduling calculations, and deployment monitoring were 
90 
 

 
demonstrated and evaluated. Feedback gathered during this stage informed adjustments 
and improvements for the succeeding sprint. 
Following the review, a Sprint Retrospective was conducted to evaluate 
development efficiency, technical issues encountered, and potential workflow 
improvements. This continuous improvement cycle strengthened system quality and 
development discipline. 
Through successive sprints, CleanConnect evolved incrementally. Initial sprints 
focused on establishing the centralized booking and database structure. Subsequent 
sprints implemented cleaner profile management and scheduling modules. Later 
iterations integrated AI-based manpower estimation and conflict detection logic. Final 
sprints refined multi-branch configuration, reporting features, and deployment 
optimization mechanisms. 
Deployment followed a controlled implementation approach. After internal sprint 
validation, the system was deployed in a staging environment to simulate operational 
conditions. User Acceptance Testing (UAT) was conducted with selected administrators 
and personnel from Cleaning Lady PH to validate real-world functionality. Upon 
approval, the system was deployed to the production environment with secured 
authentication protocols and encrypted database access. Post-deployment monitoring 
ensured stability, scheduling accuracy, and consistent multi-platform performance. 
By utilizing the Scrum framework under the Agile SDLC, CleanConnect was 
developed through structured iterations that encouraged continuous feedback, systematic 
91 
 

 
refinement, and incremental system delivery. This approach ensured that AI scheduling 
logic, manpower estimation processes, and deployment workflows were progressively 
optimized while maintaining system reliability and scalability.
